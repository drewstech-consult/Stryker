## Getting Proper Authorization

Before testing any system, ensure you have:

1. **Written authorization** — email, signed contract or bug bounty scope document
2. **Defined scope** — exactly which systems, IP ranges and domains are in scope
3. **Testing window** — agreed dates and times for testing
4. **Emergency contact** — someone who can halt testing if needed
5. **Rules of engagement** — what you can and cannot do during the engagement

A verbal "go ahead" is not sufficient. Always get it in writing.

## Responsible Disclosure

If you discover a real vulnerability during authorized testing:

1. Document the finding clearly with steps to reproduce
2. Notify the affected organization privately and promptly
3. Allow reasonable time for remediation (typically 30-90 days) before public disclosure
4. Follow any applicable bug bounty program rules
5. Do not access, copy or exfiltrate data beyond what is needed to prove the vulnerability
